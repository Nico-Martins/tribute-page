Joaquín Torres-García

Constructivo con calle y gran pez, 1946. This painting is a great example of the style this author is known for.

Joaquín was a Uruguayan artist who adhered to the universal constructivism current. He himself forged said branch of art in a thousand page manifesto. Nowadays he is mostly known for his paintings in red, white, black and blue which heavily inspire from cubism.
